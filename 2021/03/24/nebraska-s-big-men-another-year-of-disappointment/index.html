Nebraska's Big Men: Another Year of Disappointment?

2021-03-24

This is a work in progress!

Since being born in 2000, I have been raised to bleed Husker Red. It hasn’t worked out much for either football or basketball, but one weak link for at least basketball has always been our big man. Besides Isaiah Roby - who really isn’t a 5 but has played a little for OKC at center - we’ve had some pretty bad history. Too many coaches and players later, we still haven’t even won a tournament game, but I think we have some intriguing big men in 2020-2021. Derrick Walker came onto the seen after his suspension cementing himself as the starter for this year and next; Eduardo Andre looks like a promising prospect who can grow into a solid B1G big; and Yvan Ouedraogo was lost in the dust and will be transferring before next season. We’ll bring in another big in Wilhelm Breidenbach next season, who definitely fits Hoiberg’s system has been a highly touted high school player, but I’m interested to see how our current 2 guys will grow.

I’m going to use some basic _____________ in R to figure out how great these guys actually were for us this year and project these guys’ growth for the coming season(s).

currentHuskers <- players %>% filter(Season == "2020-2021", Team == "Nebraska Cornhuskers")
playersselected <- players %>% 
  filter(MP>0) %>% filter(Pos == "C") %>% filter(Season == "2020-2021") %>%
  select(Player, Team, Pos, MP, `FG%`, AST, TOV, PTS, TRB) %>% 
  na.omit() 
playersscaled <- playersselected %>% 
  select(MP, `FG%`, AST, TOV, PTS, TRB) %>% 
  mutate_all(scale) %>% 
  na.omit()
# function to compute total within-cluster sum of square 
wss <- function(k) {
  kmeans(playersscaled, k, nstart = 10 )$tot.withinss
}

# Compute and plot wss for k = 1 to k = 15
k.values <- 1:15

# extract wss for 2-15 clusters
wss_values <- map_dbl(k.values, wss)
## Warning: did not converge in 10 iterations
plot(k.values, wss_values,
       type="b", pch = 19, frame = FALSE, 
       xlab="Number of clusters K",
       ylab="Total within-clusters sum of squares")
